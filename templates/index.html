    Starting the Night
    • Must to do a rouse check for hunger
    • May heal one Aggravated Health damage doing an additional 3 rouse checks (must pass all 3 to heal)
    • Recover superficial Willpower damage equal to Resolve or Composure, whichever is higher
    Ending the Night
    • Any character with stains on their humanity must roll remorse. With a dice pool equal to the unmarked, unchecked Humanity (minimum 1 for full), any successes means Humanity level remains. All failures, and Humanity decreases by 1. Regardless of result, all stains are removed.
      (for example, Humanity level 6 with 2 stains rolls 2 dice)
    • Heal one Aggravated Willpower damage if player stuck to their Ambition the session

    Rousing the Blood
    • On failure (1 for d10), player gains 1 hunger
    • Player may augment any skill with Blood Surge by Rousing the Blood (see below)
    • Player may heal superficial and aggravated damage by Rousing the Blood (see "Each Night")
    • Player does a rouse check when waking up each night (see "Each Night")
    • Player may need to do a rouse check when in contact with blood or vitae
    • Player needs to do a rouse check to activate certain disciplines
    Blood Surge
    • Dice may be added (see Blood Potency) to a dice pool involving a single stat or attribute
    • Die is added regardless of roll
    • This extra die stays in case of Willpower re-roll
    • Lasts for the one single roll
    • Blood Surge cannot be used to boost Willpower or Humanity checks
    Winning At A Cost

    If you fail a roll but have at least one success, you may "win at a cost" which means you suffer some consequences in exchange for a success such as:
    • Take superficial damage
    • Gain Hunger
    • Attract unwanted attention
    • Lose a valuable item
    • Hurt someone
    Willpower Re-Roll

    Player may take a point of superficial Willpower damage to re-roll up to 3 regular dice.

    Teamwork

    Players working together on a task may take the largest of the players' dice pools and then add one for each player that has at least one point in the relevant ability or skill and is assisting.

    Bestial Failure

    A Bestial Failure is a failed roll where at least one die in the Hunger pool shows a 1. The character fails due to the Beast excessively manifesting, or the failure itself causes the Beast to react. Spending a point of willpower to reroll can prevent this with enough additional successes
    • Gain a Compulsion (most likely outcome)
    • Gain 1 Hunger
    • Take Aggravated damage (least likely outcome)
    Health Tracker
    • Max value is equal to Stamina + 3. The rest of the boxes get filled in.
      (Ex. Stamina = 3; Health = 3 + 3 = 6, so fill in the last 4 boxes)
    • Superficial damage is taken as single slashes in the open boxes
    • Aggravated damage is a full X, and starts filling open boxes before boxes with superficial damage
    • Any additional superficial damage taken after all boxes have slashes counts as aggravated damage
    • Superficial and Aggravated damage can be healed by rousing the blood (see "Rousing the Blood" & "Blood Potency" for Superficial, "Starting the Night" for Aggravated)
    Willpower Tracker
    • Max value equal to Composure + Resolve, and the rest of the boxes get filled in.
      (Ex. Stamina = 3, Resolve = 4; Willpower = 3 + 4 = 7, so fill in the last 3 boxes)
    • Willpower tests will consider the number of undamaged willpower, not the maximum value
    • Willpower damage works the same as health damage, as in superficial and aggravated with slashes and X's
    • Full Willpower tracker means -2 dicepool penalty Impairment for all Social and Mental rolls
    • Superficial willpower damage is taken:
      • When spending willpower to re-roll
      • In draining social situations
      • To take control of player for a single turn during frenzy
      • To make very small movements while staked through the heart
      • To ignore Health damange penalties including impairment for a single turn
    • Superficial willpower damage is recovered by:
      • See "Starting the Night" & "Ending the Night"
      • Once per session, 1 Superficial damage may be immediately removed if the player definitively acts to further their Desire (p. 174)
      • A player that plays out a Messy Crit, Bestial Failure, frenzy, or Compulsion (p. 208) can recover 1 Superficial damage for briefly satiating the Beast
    • Recover 1 or more Aggravated damage for acting to benefit a Touchstone or upholding a Conviction against their own best interest

    Blood Potency

    All Thin-bloods have a blood Potency of 0 and cannot raise it unless they Diablerize a lower Generation Vampire. Their ability to use Benefits of the Blood are limited to Blood Surge, and many Vampires do not even consider them to be Kindred. In fact, very conservative Elders do not even consider those under Blood Potency of 3 to be true Kindred.

    Vampires, otherwise, start at the minimum blood potency for their Generation (dependent on the Generation of their Sire) and increase it only through age (+1 for every 100 years, -1 for every 50 years in Torpor), experience, or Diablerie. Blood potency cannot increase above the maximum for the Generation (Generation only increases through Diablerie of a lower Generation).

    Frenzy

     The primary purpose of the Frenzy is to kill or otherwise neutralize the stimulus responsible for it, so a vampire driven to Frenzy will attempt to feed on anything she can catch whereas a character entering frenzy in combat will attempt to kill all nearby combatants.

     All difficulties to Dominate or otherwise mentally control a frenzied character are increased by two, and all difficulties to resist the effects of such mental control are reduced by two. The character never needs Willpower rolls to accomplish a feat, because the rage fueling the vampire’s actions is both a catalyst to heightened state of mind and a barrier against unwanted intrusions.

     Brujah get +2 to difficulty. Gangrel gain a physical feature characteristic of their animal as a sign of their closeness to the beast.

    • Banu Haqim
      Judgment - Banu Haqim are compelled to punish anyone seen to transgress against their personal creed, taking their blood as just vengeance for the crime. For one scene the vampire must slake at least one Hunger level from anyone, friend or foe, who acts against a personal Conviction of theirs. Failure to do so results in a three-dice penalty to all rolls until the compulsion is satisfied or the scene ends. (If the one fed from is a vampire, it also triggers their Bane.)

    • Brujah
      Rebellion - the vampire takes a stand against whatever or whomever they see as the status quo in the situation, whether that is their leader, a viewpoint expressed by a potential vessel, or just the task they were supposed to do at the moment. Until they have gone against their orders or expectations, perceived or real, the vampire receives a two dice penalty to all rolls. This Compulsion ends once they have managed to either make someone change their minds (by force if necessary) or done the opposite of what was expected of them.

    • Gangrel
      Feral Impulses - returning to an animalistic state, the vampire regresses to a point where speech is hard, clothes are uncomfortable, and arguments are best settled with teeth and claws. For one scene, the vampire gains a three-dice penalty to all rolls involving Manipulation and Intelligence. They can only speak in one-word sentences during this time.

    • Hecata
      Morbidity - a sense of morbid curiosity. The Hecata's Blood urges them to study the individuals around them for signs of illness, frailty, or impending death. Until they have either predicted a death or solved the cause of a local one, the vampire suffers a three-dice penalty to other rolls. Their conclusions do not need to be absolutely correct, but should stay within the boundaries of the possible.

    • Lasombra
      Ruthlessness - To the Lasombra, failure is not an option. Their Blood urges them to increasingly ruthless measures when the Beast flares and they are faced with failure. The next action they fail after suffering this Compulsion causes all rolls to receive a penalty until a future attempt at the same action succeeds.

    • Malkavian
      Delusion – their extrasensory gifts running wild, the vampire experiences what might be truths or portents, but what others call figments of imagination, dredged up by Hunger. While still functional, the vampire’s mind and perceptions are skewed. They receive a two-dice penalty to rolls involving Dexterity, Manipulation, Composure, and Wits as well as on rolls to resist terror frenzy, for one scene.

    • Ministry
      Transgression - Set teaches that everyone's mind and spirit are bound by invisible chains of their own making. Their Blood chafes at these bindings and the Minister suffers a burning need to break them. The vampire receives a two-dice penalty to all dice pools not relating to enticing someone (including themselves) to break a Chronicle Tenet or personal Conviction, causing at least one Stain and ending this Compulsion.

    • Nosferatu
      Cryptophilia – the need to know permeates the vampire. They become consumed with a hunger for secrets, to know that which few or no one knows, almost as strong as that for blood. They also refuse to share secrets with others, except in strict trade for greater ones.

    • Ravnos
      Tempting Fate - The Ravnos vampire is driven by their Blood to court danger. Haunted as they are by righteous fire burning its way up their lineage, why not? The next time the vampire is faced with a problem to solve, any attempt at a solution short of the most daring or dangerous incurs a two-dice penalty. (Suitably flashy and risky attempts can even merit bonus dice for this occasion.) The Daredevil is free to convince any fellows to do things their way, but is just as likely to go at it alone. The Compulsion persists until the problem is solved or further attempts become impossible.

    • Salubri
      Affective Empathy - When a Salubri suffers a Compulsion, the Kindred becomes overwhelmed with empathy for a personal problem that afflicts someone in the scene, seeking to further its resolution. The scale of the personal problem isn’t important; the Salubri understands that sometimes suffering is part of a cumulative situation and not an isolated stimulus. Any action not taken toward mitigating that personal tragedy incurs a two dice penalty. The Compulsion persists until the sufferer’s burden is eased or a more immediate crisis supersedes it, or the end of the scene.

    • Toreador
      Obsession - enraptured by beauty, the vampire becomes temporarily obsessed with a singular gorgeous thing, able to think of nothing else. Pick one feature, such as a person, a song, an artwork, blood spatter, or even a sunrise. Enraptured, the vampire can hardly take their attention from it, and if spoken to, they only talk about that subject. Any other actions receive a two-dice penalty. This Compulsion lasts until they can no longer perceive the beloved object or the scene ends.

    • Tremere
      Perfectionism - nothing but the best satisfies the vampire. Anything less than exceptional performance instills a profound sense of failure, and they often repeat tasks obsessively to get them "just right". Until the vampire scores a critical win on a Skill roll or the scene ends, the vampire labors under a two-dice penalty to all dice pools. The penalty is reduced to one die for a repeated action and removed entirely on a second repeat.

    • Tzimisce
      Covetousness - when a Tzimisce suffers a Compulsion, the Kindred becomes obsessed with possessing something in the scene, desiring to add it to their proverbial hoard. This can be anything from an object to a piece of property to an actual person. Any action not taken toward this purpose incurs a two-dice penalty. The Compulsion persists until ownership is established (the Storyteller decides what constitutes ownership in the case of a non-object) or the object of desire becomes unattainable.

    • Ventrue
      Arrogance - the need to rule rears its head in the vampire. They stop at nothing to assume command of a situation. Someone must obey an order from the vampire. Any action not directly associated with leadership receives a two-dice penalty. This Compulsion lasts until an order has been obeyed, though the order must not be supernaturally enforced, such as through Dominate.
