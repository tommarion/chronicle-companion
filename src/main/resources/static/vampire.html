Starting the Night
  • Must to do a rouse check for hunger
  • May heal Aggravated damage to Health (# equal to Blood Potency) doing an additional 3 rouse checks
  • Recover superficial Willpower damage equal to Resolve or Composure, whichever is higher
Ending the Night
  • Any character with stains on their humanity must roll remorse. With a dice pool equal to the unmarked, unchecked Humanity (minimum 1 for full), any successes means Humanity level remains. All failures, and Humanity decreases by 1. Regardless of result, all stains are removed.
    (for example, Humanity level 6 with 2 stains rolls 2 dice)
  • Heal one Aggravated Willpower damage if player stuck to their Ambition the session
  • Characters that survive the events of the evening improve their chances for surviving the next one, hence, they each get 1 EXP point. The end of a story means an additional EXP point as well.

Rousing the Blood
  • On failure (1 for d10), player gains 1 hunger
  • Player may augment any skill with Blood Surge by Rousing the Blood (see below)
  • Player may heal superficial and aggravated damage by Rousing the Blood (see "Each Night")
  • Player does a rouse check when waking up each night (see "Each Night")
  • Player may need to do a rouse check when in contact with blood or vitae
  • Player needs to do a rouse check to activate certain disciplines
Blood Surge
  • Dice may be added (see Blood Potency) to a dice pool involving a single stat or attribute
  • Die is added regardless of roll
  • This extra die stays in case of Willpower re-roll
  • Lasts for the one single roll
  • Blood Surge cannot be used to boost Willpower or Humanity checks
Winning At A Cost

If you fail a roll but have at least one success, you may "win at a cost" which means you suffer some consequences in exchange for a success such as:
  • Take superficial damage
  • Gain Hunger
  • Attract unwanted attention
  • Lose a valuable item
  • Hurt someone
Willpower Re-Roll

Player may take a point of superficial Willpower damage to re-roll up to 3 regular dice.

Teamwork

Players working together on a task may take the largest of the players' dice pools and then add one for each player that has at least one point in the relevant ability or skill and is assisting.

Bestial Failure

A Bestial Failure is a failed roll where at least one die in the Hunger pool shows a 1. The character fails due to the Beast excessively manifesting, or the failure itself causes the Beast to react. Spending a point of willpower to reroll can prevent this with enough additional successes
  • Gain a Compulsion (most likely outcome)
  • Gain 1 Hunger
  • Take Aggravated damage (least likely outcome)
Health Tracker
  • Max value is equal to Stamina + 3. The rest of the boxes get filled in.
    (Ex. Stamina = 3; Health = 3 + 3 = 6, so fill in the last 4 boxes)
  • Superficial damage is taken as single slashes in the open boxes
  • Aggravated damage is a full X, and starts filling open boxes before boxes with superficial damage
  • Any additional superficial damage taken after all boxes have slashes counts as aggravated damage
  • Superficial and Aggravated damage can be healed by rousing the blood (see "Rousing the Blood" & "Blood Potency" for Superficial, "Starting the Night" for Aggravated)
Willpower Tracker
  • Max value equal to Composure + Resolve, and the rest of the boxes get filled in.
    (Ex. Stamina = 3, Resolve = 4; Willpower = 3 + 4 = 7, so fill in the last 3 boxes)
  • Willpower tests will consider the number of undamaged willpower, not the maximum value
  • Willpower damage works the same as health damage, as in superficial and aggravated with slashes and X's
  • Full Willpower tracker means -2 dicepool penalty Impairment for all Social and Mental rolls
  • Superficial willpower damage is taken:
    • When spending willpower to re-roll
    • In draining social situations
    • To take control of player for a single turn during frenzy
    • To make very small movements while staked through the heart
    • To ignore Health damange penalties including impairment for a single turn
  • Superficial willpower damage is recovered by:
    • See "Starting the Night" & "Ending the Night"
    • Once per session, 1 Superficial damage may be immediately removed if the player definitively acts to further their Desire (p. 174)
    • A player that plays out a Messy Crit, Bestial Failure, frenzy, or Compulsion (p. 208) can recover 1 Superficial damage for briefly satiating the Beast
  • Recover 1 or more Aggravated damage for acting to benefit a Touchstone or upholding a Conviction against their own best interest
Humanity Tracker
  • Humanity Level is marked directly
    (Ex. Humanity = 7, fill in the first 7 boxes)
  • Tenet violations cause Stains, which act like Superficial damage marked as slashes in boxes from right to left (there is no Aggravated)
  • Tenet violations committed in order to uphold a Conviction get a reduction in the number of Stains incurred

  • Most fledgelings will start off with a Humanity level of 7
  • Humanity level of 10 is rare even among humans
  • Humanity level dropping to 0 means they are lost to the Beast, attributes are increased by 5 in a frenzy, and they become a wight
  • Humanity & Stains are lost by Remorse rolls (see "Ending the Night")
  • Humanity can be regained by major personal story arc events such as:
    • Gaining a new Touchstone
    • Deliberate turning away from Kindred society and power
    • Actions such as non-violently defending a museum or hospital
Blood Potency

All Thin-bloods have a blood Potency of 0 and cannot raise it unless they Diablerize a lower Generation Vampire. Their ability to use Benefits of the Blood are limited to Blood Surge, and many Vampires do not even consider them to be Kindred. In fact, very conservative Elders do not even consider those under Blood Potency of 3 to be true Kindred.

Vampires, otherwise, start at the minimum blood potency for their Generation (dependent on the Generation of their Sire) and increase it only through age (+1 for every 100 years, -1 for every 50 years in Torpor), experience, or Diablerie. Blood potency cannot increase above the maximum for the Generation (Generation only increases through Diablerie of a lower Generation).

Frenzy

 The primary purpose of the Frenzy is to kill or otherwise neutralize the stimulus responsible for it, so a vampire driven to Frenzy will attempt to feed on anything she can catch whereas a character entering frenzy in combat will attempt to kill all nearby combatants.

 All difficulties to Dominate or otherwise mentally control a frenzied character are increased by two, and all difficulties to resist the effects of such mental control are reduced by two. The character never needs Willpower rolls to accomplish a feat, because the rage fueling the vampireâ€™s actions is both a catalyst to heightened state of mind and a barrier against unwanted intrusions.

 Brujah get +2 to difficulty. Gangrel gain a physical feature characteristic of their animal as a sign of their closeness to the beast.

  • Hunger torpor: feeding the vampire will wake them up
  • Damage torpor: the vampire will be out for a predetermined amount of time determined by humanity, the blood of a lower gen will cut that time.
  • Staked: as long as the stake is in the heart that vampire is paralyzed, nothing will wake it besides pulling the stake. Additionally that vampire is also aware of its surroundings unlike any other form of torpor.

Animalism
Masquerade Threat: Low - Medium
  • Bound Famulus: The vampire can form a bond with an animal facilitating the use of other Animalism powers.
    • Cost: Must feed animal blood 3 separate nights (rouse check each time)
    • Dice Pool: Charisma + Animal Ken

  • Sense the Beast: The vampire can sense the beast within other vampires, mortals and supernatural creatures.
    • Cost: Free
    • Dice Pool: Wits + Animalism vs Composure + Subterfuge
    • System: Sense level of hostility, and presence of the Beast. Critical - Can tell exactly what type of being and hunger level. Can be used passively to detect supernatural beings/enemies.
  • Feral Whispers: The vampire can summon and have two-way communication with an area-appropriate animal. With high enough skill, the animal can even be pursuaded to perform simple tasks.
    • Cost: One Rouse Check per type of animal per scene. Allows one summoning and unlimited communication (famulus is free).
    • Dice Pool: Manipulation + Animalism (persuasion)
      Charisma + Animalism (summoning)
  • Animal Succulence: The vampire can slake additional Hunger when feeding from animals. The vampire may also consume their famulus for a large amount of nourishment as well as absorbing some of its powers.
    • Cost: Free
    • Feeding slakes one additional Hunger, and the vampire may count their Blood Potency as 2 less in terms of feeding penalties from animals. Consuming the famulus slakes 4 hunger (but never the final point) and gains 2 extra dots in one relevant attribute until hunger 5 or the next feeding.

  • Quell the Beast: By locking eyes with a target, the vampire is able to subdue their inner beast into temporary slumber. Mortals become apathetic becoming unable to do anything other than simply survive whereas vampires' bestial urges temporarily abate, for better or worse.
    • Cost: One Rouse Check
    • Dice Pool: Charisma + Animalism vs Stamina + Resolve
    • System: A win against a mortal incapacitates them for the scene. A win against a vampire prevents Blood Surges and Messy Crits lasting the number of turns as the margin of success of the roll, and can end a Frenzy on a Critical Win.

  • Unliving Hive: Most often used by the Nosferatu animal powers can extend to swarms of insects like flies and roaches. They may even become famulus and dwell within the folds of their malformed flesh.
    • Amalgam: Obfuscate 2
    • Cost: Free
    • System: This power allows other animal powers to extend to swarms of insects, treating them as a single animal. They have Health 5 and can resist an attack with a pool of 8. They are useful for spying and incapacitating enemies. They take aggravated damage from flames and insecticide.
Auspex
Masquerade Threat: Very Low
  • Heightened Senses: The vampire can sharpen their senses to a preternatural degree, giving them the ability to see in pitch darkness, hear ultrasonic frequencies, smell the cowering fear of prey, etc.
    • Cost: Free
    • Dice Pool: Wits + Resolve
    • System: User can add Auspex rating to all relevant perception rolls. If there is an intense sensation relevant to the boosted sense, they must make a Wits + Resolve roll to dampen their senses in time, otherwise take a -3 penalty to all perception rolls for the rest of the scene.

  • Sense the Unseen: The senses of the vampire become attuned to dimensions beyond the mundane, allowing them to sense presences otherwise hidden from the naken eye. This can be vampires using Obfuscate, ghosts, and even latent Blood Sorcery.
    • Cost: Free
    • Dice Pool: Wits + Auspex or Resolve + Auspex
    • System: The storyteller can call for a blind roll of Wits + Auspex against Wits + Obfuscate for an entity actively trying to stay hidden. If the User is actively searching, use Resolve + Auspex.
  • Premonition: The vampire experiences flashes of insight. They may take the form of raised hackles, sudden inspiration or even vivid visions. While never exactly precise, they can nudge the user out of harms way or to the solution of an elusive problem.
    • Cost: One Rouse Check when actively used
    • Dice Pool: Wits + Resolve
    • System: The vampire may randomly get flashes of some kind at story-appropriate moments. They may also actively provoke a premonition by focusing on a subject, making a Rouse Check, and then rolling Resolve + Auspex to determine the level of insight on the subject.
  • Scry the Soul: By focusing on a person, the vampire can perceive the state of the person's psyche as a shifting aura of colors. Auras reveal little precise information but do provide clues regarding many subjects. The vampire can scan a crowd if looking for a specific condition; size of crows determines difficulty.
    • Cost: One Rouse Check
    • Dice Pool: Intelligence + Auspex vs Composure + Subterfuge
    • System: Answer a number of questions (equal to the margin of success):
      • Emotional state of subject
      • Resonance of subjects blood
      • Whether the subject is a Vampire, Werewolf, Ghoul, or any other supernatural being
      • Whether the subject is under the influence of any Blood Sorcery or other magic
      • Whether the subject has committed diablerie within the past year
      • A Critical Win reveals something unexpected

  • Share the Senses: The vampire can tap into the senses of another mortal or vampire, seeing, hearing, and feeling what they do. The user also retains their own senses as well. They can choose which senses they wish to tap into. When used on a stranger it requires line-of-sight, but can be used over longer distances on someone with the user's blood in their body.
    • Cost: Free
    • Dice Pool: Resolve + Auspex
    • System: Roll Resolve + Auspex with Difficulty 3. This can change depending on distractions, distance and other circumstances. This effect usually goes unnotices on the target but can be noticed with Sense the Unseen. Wits + Resolve match can kick out the user, in which case they cannot be targeted until the next night.

Blood Sorcery Rituals
Masquerade Threat: High

Ritual Roll: Intelligence + Blood Sorcery
(with difficulty = Ritual Level + 1)
  • Blood Walk: Expands the power of A Taste for Blood allowing the user to learn more details about the subject studied, assuming the subject is a vampire.
    • Ingredients: A silver cup filled with Blood from the subject. (One Rouse Check's worth)
    • Process: The user mixes their vitae with the subject's and repeats an incantation over the cup, spending roughly an hour.
    • Dice Pool: Ritual Roll
    • System: Allows the user to learn the generation, and name of subject, as well as the subject's sire and clan. A Critical Win also informs the user of any blood bonds or effective Blood Sorcery involving the subject.

  • Clinging of the Insect: The vampire gains the ability to cling to walls like a grotesque insect or spider.
    • Ingredients: A living spider.
    • Process: The caster fills a vial with their own vitae and crushes the spider into it, mixing the ground bits with the vitae before ingesting it. This does not slake any hunger. This ritual only works for the caster, others gain no benefit
    • System: A successful ritual roll gives the ability to cling to walls for an entire scene. A Critical Win grants the effect for the entire night. Must cling with both hands and feet and move at about half speed.

  • Craft Bloodstone: A bloodstone is a magical tracker in which the creator always knows the direction and general distance of.
    • Ingredients: A small pebble of iron or a small magnet, as well as a liter of blood from any source in a silver bowl.
    • Process: The user spills their vitae into the blood in the bowl and then recites a chant over it for an hour, repeating the chant over the next two nights. The pebble, or magnet, absorbs the blood over the 3 nights; upon successful completion the liquid appears translucent.
    • System: Make the Ritual Roll at the end of the 3rd night. On a win, the caster attunes their mind to the Bloodstone. This lasts for a week or until the Bloodstone is destroyed. The user can have as many Bloodstones as their Resolve value.

  • Wake with Evening's Freshness: Performed before dawn, this ritual allows the caster to awake at any sign of danger, fully alert as if awake during the night.
    • Ingredients: Burnt bones and feathers of a rooster.
    • Process: The caster mixes the ashes with their vitae drawing a circle around their place of sleep.
    • System: Ritual roll only when in danger. Ignore daytime penalties. Effect lasts until following dawn on Critical Win.

  • Ward Against Ghouls: Warding rules on p275
    • Cost: Caster's blood and an object to set the ward to.

    • System: No penalty for darkness. Supernatural appearance adds two bonus dice to Intimidation rolls against humans.

  • Weight of the Feather: The vampire can reduce their effective mass and density, making themselves almost weightless. This allows them to avoid triggering pressure sensors as well as avoiding major damage from falls, collisions, or being thrown. The power cannot be used for longer leaps, as the vampireâ€™s strength is proportionally reduced.
    • Cost: Free
    • Dice Pool: Wits + Survival
    • System: If prepared, no roll is required. Falling is Difficulty 3. Will forego fall, collision, and throw damage. Will not trigger pressure sensitive sensors

  • Feral Weapons: The vampire can extend their natural weapons to monstrous proportions. This usually takes the form of fingernails extending into wicked talons but can also come in other forms such as fangs elongating into veritable daggers, as from a gigantic serpent.
    • Cost: One rouse check
    • System: Weapon becomes Brawl weapon with +2 modifier. Bites still only deal 2 Aggravated damage. Superficial damage taken by this weapon is not halved while active.

  • Earth Meld: Becoming one with the soil, the vampire sinks into the earth. Unless in torpor, the vampire rises again the following night.
    • Cost: One rouse check
    • System: Must be on natural earth. Takes one full turn and items are left on the ground above.

  • Shapechange: The vampire can assume the shape of an animal roughly the same size as their original mass. The vampire can only change into one type of animal (usually a wolf, sometimes a large feline or a giant snake), usually one associated with their clan or the type of prey they most commonly feed on. The animal, while usually a spectacular example of their species, shows no signs to a mundane observer of being supernatural.
    • Cost: One rouse check
    • System: Takes one full turn and no other actions may be used. Gains attributes of taken animal (p. 373 in core book). Can still use Disciplines.

Damage
  • All superficial damage is halved (round up), except where spent (i.e. Willpower re-rolls)
  • When Superficial damage tracker is full, take -2 to all dice pools. Any additional Superficial damage counts as Aggravated
  • Aggravated damange also adds Superficial damange, unless Superficial damage tracker is full
  • When Aggravated damange tracker is full, mortals are dead and Vampires are in torpor
  • Two-sided combat: (winners successes - losers successes) + weapon modifier = damage
  • Tie results in both getting damaged with success margin of 1
  • Dexterity + Athletics for dodge, no damage inflicted to attacker. Consider cover modifer (-2 for none)

Types
  • Brawl / Non-Lethal Melee Weapons - Superficial damage
  • Lethal Melee Weapons / Firearms - Aggravated damage for humans, superficial for vampires
  • Sunlight / Fire / Supernatural Claws & Teeth - Aggravated damage for Vampires (fire & Sun - chance to frenzy).

Healing
  • Superficial health damage can be healed once per turn with rouse check.
  • Aggravated health damage can be healed with 3 additional rouse checks only once per night when waking up.

  • Banu Haqim
    Judgment - Banu Haqim are compelled to punish anyone seen to transgress against their personal creed, taking their blood as just vengeance for the crime. For one scene the vampire must slake at least one Hunger level from anyone, friend or foe, who acts against a personal Conviction of theirs. Failure to do so results in a three-dice penalty to all rolls until the compulsion is satisfied or the scene ends. (If the one fed from is a vampire, it also triggers their Bane.)

  • Brujah
    Rebellion - the vampire takes a stand against whatever or whomever they see as the status quo in the situation, whether that is their leader, a viewpoint expressed by a potential vessel, or just the task they were supposed to do at the moment. Until they have gone against their orders or expectations, perceived or real, the vampire receives a two dice penalty to all rolls. This Compulsion ends once they have managed to either make someone change their minds (by force if necessary) or done the opposite of what was expected of them.

  • Gangrel
    Feral Impulses - returning to an animalistic state, the vampire regresses to a point where speech is hard, clothes are uncomfortable, and arguments are best settled with teeth and claws. For one scene, the vampire gains a three-dice penalty to all rolls involving Manipulation and Intelligence. They can only speak in one-word sentences during this time.

  • Hecata
    Morbidity - a sense of morbid curiosity. The Hecata's Blood urges them to study the individuals around them for signs of illness, frailty, or impending death. Until they have either predicted a death or solved the cause of a local one, the vampire suffers a three-dice penalty to other rolls. Their conclusions do not need to be absolutely correct, but should stay within the boundaries of the possible.

  • Lasombra
    Ruthlessness - To the Lasombra, failure is not an option. Their Blood urges them to increasingly ruthless measures when the Beast flares and they are faced with failure. The next action they fail after suffering this Compulsion causes all rolls to receive a penalty until a future attempt at the same action succeeds.

  • Malkavian
    Delusion â€“ their extrasensory gifts running wild, the vampire experiences what might be truths or portents, but what others call figments of imagination, dredged up by Hunger. While still functional, the vampireâ€™s mind and perceptions are skewed. They receive a two-dice penalty to rolls involving Dexterity, Manipulation, Composure, and Wits as well as on rolls to resist terror frenzy, for one scene.

  • Ministry
    Transgression - Set teaches that everyone's mind and spirit are bound by invisible chains of their own making. Their Blood chafes at these bindings and the Minister suffers a burning need to break them. The vampire receives a two-dice penalty to all dice pools not relating to enticing someone (including themselves) to break a Chronicle Tenet or personal Conviction, causing at least one Stain and ending this Compulsion.

  • Nosferatu
    Cryptophilia â€“ the need to know permeates the vampire. They become consumed with a hunger for secrets, to know that which few or no one knows, almost as strong as that for blood. They also refuse to share secrets with others, except in strict trade for greater ones.

  • Ravnos
    Tempting Fate - The Ravnos vampire is driven by their Blood to court danger. Haunted as they are by righteous fire burning its way up their lineage, why not? The next time the vampire is faced with a problem to solve, any attempt at a solution short of the most daring or dangerous incurs a two-dice penalty. (Suitably flashy and risky attempts can even merit bonus dice for this occasion.) The Daredevil is free to convince any fellows to do things their way, but is just as likely to go at it alone. The Compulsion persists until the problem is solved or further attempts become impossible.

  • Salubri
    Affective Empathy - When a Salubri suffers a Compulsion, the Kindred becomes overwhelmed with empathy for a personal problem that afflicts someone in the scene, seeking to further its resolution. The scale of the personal problem isnâ€™t important; the Salubri understands that sometimes suffering is part of a cumulative situation and not an isolated stimulus. Any action not taken toward mitigating that personal tragedy incurs a two dice penalty. The Compulsion persists until the suffererâ€™s burden is eased or a more immediate crisis supersedes it, or the end of the scene.

  • Toreador
    Obsession - enraptured by beauty, the vampire becomes temporarily obsessed with a singular gorgeous thing, able to think of nothing else. Pick one feature, such as a person, a song, an artwork, blood spatter, or even a sunrise. Enraptured, the vampire can hardly take their attention from it, and if spoken to, they only talk about that subject. Any other actions receive a two-dice penalty. This Compulsion lasts until they can no longer perceive the beloved object or the scene ends.

  • Tremere
    Perfectionism - nothing but the best satisfies the vampire. Anything less than exceptional performance instills a profound sense of failure, and they often repeat tasks obsessively to get them "just right".
